The twist of the week: how do colors affect social media choices?

The Twist · March 2026

Colors has always been playing a paramount role in one’s life: whether in choosing what movie to watch or what dish to cook- subconsciously we choose the product portraying our personality or mindset at that moment. Why so? Follow up and we will explain everything!

Marketing behind the colors:

Interruption of doomscrolling

Nowadays, people do very little during their weekends or Friday evenings except scrolling through TikTok and see thousands of boring videos daily. For that matter, coming across an eye-catching colorful advertisement immediately draws their attention and persuades their brain to focus on the first unique video in a 4-hour-long-scrolling loop. This usually causes the increase of sparks of curiosity towards the brand, as well as becoming a new follower of one. For example, let’s imagine that Coca Cola is only starting their career and do not have half of population in the word buying their product. So, imagine you are mindlessly scrolling through social media and every video you see is the same: dim and basic. When all of a sudden you stumble upon a video presenting vibrant red cans of a beverage. You stop, watch it and, maybe, follow the brand. And boom- because of the memorable color-red- and thorough presentation a brand like Coca Cola earns a new follower.

Purchases driven by emotions

While making purchases, colors not only affect, but determine the future of our money. When browsing through TikTok and seeing a cool add of vibrant clothing, it always is ‘Ohhh, it would look so good on me’, and never ‘Do I really need it?’ type of thoughts that crawl to the back of our heads. And brands use it as an ace in their sleeve. Building a color-based brand identity allows companies to attract the right target audience that aligns with the concept of a brand. Red is associated with fun and danger, driving customers to make impulsive purchases. Whereas, black represents luxury and exclusivity, causing individual to think highly of the brand and the need to be a part of an elite world. In most of the cases, the purchases are emotional, not rational: one’s emotional state overweights the price of the product.

The bottom line is, the success of the advirtisment, whether for a campaign or a product, is determined by the colors in the media and what they make people feel.

Psychology behind colours and consumer behavior

From a psychological and biological view, colors influence human perceptions long before consciousness kicks in. According to color psychology research, the brain processes visual information in as little as 13 milliseconds. So, while you haven’t even comprehended the information on your screen, your brain had already processed the colours in the image and reacted to triggers, caused by colors

Emotional responses

Studies in consumer psychology state, that up to 85% of purchasing decisions can be influences by color perception. Colors stimulate different areas of brain, while activating emotional and physiological responses. For example, red increases heart rate and can cause a sense of urgency, while blu promotes trust, safety and calmness. All these reactions are not accidental, but rooted in evolutionary biology and cultural conditioning. Red is historically associated with danger( fire, blood), which is why it quickly captures attention.

If we will look from the side of neuroscience, researches show that much of decision- making is happening in subconscious mind. When we scroll through Tik Tok feed, our brain remains in a mode of automatic processing. We do not analyze the videos thoroughly; instead, our brain reacts to color stimulation, that stands out. Bright colors interrupt patterns. When our brain detects something visually different from the surrounding content, it works like a hook . The brain releases dopamine and creates a micro moment of curiosity. That is why colorful advertisements break the “doomscrolling cycle”.

So, colors are far more than conscious choices. They are kind of psychological tools, that create perception, interrupt our brain responses and make us buy more and give the brand an opportunity to grip our attention. Whether we are choosing what to eat or which long sleeve to buy, colours subconsciously determine our decisions.
